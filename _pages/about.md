***

I'm Connor Colombe. I recently completed my PhD in Operations Research at the University of Texas at Austin (2026), where I was advised by Professors [Benjamin D. Leibowicz](https://sites.utexas.edu/leibowicz/) and [J. Eric Bickel](https://www.me.utexas.edu/people/faculty-directory/bickel). I received my B.S. in physics and mathematics from [Harvey Mudd College](https://www.hmc.edu/) in 2018, and my M.S. in Computer Science from the University of Texas at Dallas in 2020, where I worked with Professor [Emily Kyle Fox](https://ekfox.web.illinois.edu) on [fast approximation algorithms for the continuous Fréchet distance](https://arxiv.org/abs/2007.07994).

My doctoral research spanned several projects: a two-stage stochastic program studying the [effects of policy uncertainty and risk aversion](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0301421524002325) on CCUS investment; a [Stackelberg game analysis](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5168632) of optimal carbon capture subsidy values; and developing a novel family of [quantile-parameterized distributions](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5930859) for use in decision analysis. I've also worked on optimizing electric grid resilience in different network topologies. 

More broadly, I'm interested in systems modeling and optimization, game theory, algorithm design, and applied probability. Outside of research, I enjoy rock climbing and recreational math and programming. You can find me doing the former on [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/connorcolombe/), and check out my [blog](https://ccolombe12.github.io/blog) for the latter!
